Archery 2025: Learning the Art of Bowhunting

September 2025 Colorado Backcountry 5-Day Hunt

The Decision to Learn Archery

After years of rifle hunting, I'd always been intrigued by archery season. There's something pure about getting close enough to an elk that you can hear its breathing, feel the tension in the air, and know that your shot has to be perfect. This year, I finally decided to take the plunge and learn bowhunting.

What I didn't expect was how this decision would completely transform my gear requirements. When you're carrying a bow, arrows, and all the extra equipment that comes with archery hunting, every piece of gear becomes critical. This hunt became the ultimate test of my three most trusted pieces of equipment - and by the end, I understood why serious hunters invest in premium gear.

Building the Perfect Archery Setup

Preparing for archery season meant completely rethinking my gear. I needed a pack that could handle extra weight and organization, a shelter that wouldn't fail in mountain weather, and a way to stay warm during those brutal early morning sits. After extensive research, I invested in three pieces of gear that would prove absolutely essential.

The Pack: Kuiu PRO 3600

The Kuiu PRO 3600 Pack became my command center. With archery equipment, I was carrying 15+ pounds more gear than usual - bow, quiver, arrows, rangefinder, calls, and extra layers for those cold morning sits. This pack's compartment system kept everything organized and accessible, while the frame distributed the weight so well I could still make those critical silent stalks.

The Shelter: PEAX Dyneema Solitude 4

For base camp, I chose the PEAX Dyneema Solitude 4 Tipi. In the high country, weather can change in minutes, and I needed a shelter I could trust completely. The Dyneema fabric and tipi design meant I could focus on hunting instead of worrying about my shelter failing during a storm.

The Heat Source: Winnerwell Stove

The Winnerwell Fastfold Titanium Stove was my secret weapon. Archery hunting means longer sits, colder mornings, and the need to be absolutely ready when opportunity presents itself. Having a reliable heat source meant I could dry gear, warm up between hunts, and maintain peak performance even in challenging conditions.

Day 1: Testing Gear Under Pressure

The first morning started at 4:30 AM with temperatures at 28°F. I fired up the Winnerwell stove and had my tipi heated to 65°F within 15 minutes - crucial for staying limber and alert during gear prep. The stove's flat top let me heat water for coffee while I organized my pack for the day's hunt.

Loading the Kuiu pack with all my archery gear, I was impressed by how the weight disappeared once properly adjusted. The pack's design kept my arrows secure but accessible, while the frame prevented any shifting that might make noise during a stalk.

The First Encounter

We spotted elk around 600 yards away in a meadow. The three-hour stalk that followed tested every piece of gear I was carrying. The pack moved with me through deadfall and steep terrain without making a sound. When we finally got to 50 yards - just outside my comfort range - I realized this hunt would demand everything from both me and my equipment.

That evening, returning to a warm tipi after 12 hours in the field felt like luxury. The Winnerwell stove quickly dried my damp gear and provided the warmth needed to maintain focus for the next day's hunting.

Days 2-4: When Weather Tests Everything

Day three brought a weather system that would test every piece of gear to its limits. Overnight, six inches of snow fell, winds gusted to 45 mph, and temperatures dropped to 15°F. This is where the value of premium gear became crystal clear.

Shelter Performance in the Storm

While other hunters were packing up and heading home, the PEAX tipi handled the storm like it was built for exactly these conditions. The Dyneema fabric shed snow and rain effortlessly, while the tipi design remained rock-solid in high winds. Inside, we stayed completely dry and comfortable.

The Winnerwell stove was absolutely critical during this storm. Not only did it keep us warm, but it allowed us to dry wet gear throughout the night. Wet equipment in these conditions isn't just uncomfortable - it can be dangerous. The stove's reliability meant we could continue hunting while others were forced to retreat.

Pack Performance in Difficult Conditions

Hunting in fresh snow with a full archery setup meant carrying extra weight and dealing with challenging terrain. The Kuiu pack's frame system distributed 50+ pounds so effectively that I could still move quietly and maintain balance during critical stalking situations. The waterproof fabric kept my gear dry even during heavy snow and rain.

We had multiple close encounters during these challenging days, and each one reinforced why quality gear matters. When you might only get one shot opportunity in five days, you need equipment that performs flawlessly under pressure.

Day 5: When Everything Comes Together

The final day started with perfect conditions - fresh snow, calm winds, and elk that were moving predictably. After warming up in the heated tipi and loading my perfectly organized pack, I felt completely confident in my equipment and abilities.

The Perfect Stalk

The final stalk lasted 90 minutes and covered nearly two miles of challenging terrain. The Kuiu pack performed flawlessly - no shifting weight, no noise, and easy access to my rangefinder and calls when needed. When the shot opportunity finally came at 32 yards, I was steady, comfortable, and ready.

The Pack-Out Performance Test

After the successful harvest, the real test began. Field dressing and quartering an elk with a full archery setup already in your pack is challenging, but the Kuiu's expandable design and load-carrying capacity handled everything. I was able to pack 60+ pounds of meat while maintaining the balance and comfort needed for the 4-mile pack-out.

Back at camp, the Winnerwell stove provided the heat needed to properly care for the meat while we processed and prepared for the journey home. Having a reliable heat source for cooking and gear maintenance made the entire process more efficient and enjoyable.

Why This Gear Investment Paid Off

The Complete System Approach

What made this hunt successful wasn't just having one piece of good gear - it was having a complete system where every piece worked together perfectly:

The Kuiu PRO 3600 Pack carried everything I needed while keeping me mobile and quiet during stalks.

The PEAX Dyneema Solitude 4 Tipi provided bombproof shelter that let us hunt through weather that sent others home.

The Investment That Keeps Paying Dividends

This archery season taught me that premium gear isn't an expense - it's an investment in success. The total cost of these three pieces of equipment was significant, but consider this: I've now used them on 15+ hunts, and they've performed flawlessly every time. That breaks down to less than $30 per hunt for gear that can make the difference between success and failure.

More importantly, this gear gives me the confidence to book more challenging hunts, stay out in worse weather, and pursue opportunities that hunters with inferior equipment simply can't attempt. The return on investment isn't just measured in dollars - it's measured in experiences and memories that last a lifetime.

If you're serious about archery hunting, don't handicap yourself with gear that might let you down when it matters most. Invest in equipment that will expand your capabilities and ensure you're ready for whatever the mountains throw at you.
